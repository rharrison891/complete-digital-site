What's Changing in Digital TV

Freeview HD & DVB-T2 Updates +

Freeview continues to shift more channels to HD broadcasting. Many SD channels are gradually being phased out, and the platform is moving toward DVB-T2 transmission for improved efficiency and higher-quality HD content.

Key changes:

  • More HD channels replacing SD equivalents
  • Older non-HD TVs may require a Freeview HD box
  • New multiplex restructuring to improve coverage
Growth of Streaming Services +

Streaming has rapidly become the preferred way to watch TV. Major platforms like Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, Apple TV+, and Paramount+ continue to expand with 4K HDR as standard on many devices.

Trends for 2025:

  • Higher adoption of smart TVs
  • More 4K and HDR content availability
  • Sharper compression for improved streaming quality
8K TVs – The Next Big Step +

8K televisions are becoming more affordable, but widespread 8K content isn’t here yet. Upscaling technology in new TVs can dramatically improve HD and 4K material, making 8K sets useful even now.

What’s happening:

  • Stronger 8K upscaling using AI processing
  • Growing support in high-end streaming devices
  • Future-focused investment as broadcasters test 8K
Satellite (Freesat & Sky) Updates +

Freesat and Sky continue improving HD channels and simplifying regional services. Sky Glass and Sky Stream have reduced reliance on dishes, with streaming-based viewing becoming the new norm.

  • More HD channel migration
  • Sky Glass / Stream replacing traditional satellite kits
  • Improved channel organisation and region detection
BBC, ITVX & General Platform Changes +

The BBC and ITV continue to push more services online. BBC iPlayer offers higher bitrate HD content, while ITVX provides a modern streaming alternative to the older ITV Hub.

  • Better HD quality on iPlayer
  • ITVX replacing legacy apps on most smart TVs
  • More connected services integrated directly into TV menus
