4K Options

What is 4K Ultra HD? ▸

4K Ultra HD offers four times the resolution of standard Full HD, giving you sharper detail, clearer textures, and more lifelike images. Most modern TVs support 4K, and many also feature HDR (High Dynamic Range), which boosts brightness and colour accuracy.

  • 3840 × 2160 resolution
  • Improved clarity, detail, and colour
  • Often paired with HDR10, HLG, or Dolby Vision
  • Requires a stable broadband connection for streaming
Streaming in 4K ▸

Most major streaming services offer a wide range of 4K content, including films, documentaries, TV shows, and live events. Some platforms require a premium subscription for Ultra HD quality.

  • Netflix – requires the Premium plan for 4K and supports HDR
  • Disney+ – many films and series available in 4K HDR
  • Amazon Prime Video – 4K included at no extra cost
  • Apple TV+ – nearly all content delivered in 4K HDR
  • YouTube – large selection of 4K clips and creator content

Recommended speed: 25Mbps+ for stable 4K streaming

Ultra HD Blu-ray ▸

For the highest possible image and audio quality, Ultra HD Blu-ray discs are still unmatched. They offer higher bitrates than streaming and often include advanced audio formats such as Dolby Atmos and DTS:X.

  • Highest-quality 4K playback available
  • No buffering or broadband needed
  • Supports HDR10, HDR10+, and Dolby Vision
  • Requires a 4K Blu-ray player or compatible console
4K Broadcast & Freely ▸

Traditional broadcast 4K channels are still limited in the UK, but satellite providers, sports broadcasters, and emerging platforms like Freely are bringing more 4K options to viewers.

  • Sky Q – dedicated 4K movies, sports, and entertainment
  • BT Sport / TNT Sports – live 4K sports broadcasts
  • Freely – future-ready platform with hybrid delivery
  • Streaming sports apps – increasing 4K event coverage
Gaming in 4K ▸

Modern consoles and gaming PCs offer excellent 4K gaming experiences, especially when paired with TVs that support high refresh rates and variable refresh rate (VRR) technologies.

  • PlayStation 5 – 4K at up to 120Hz on supported TVs
  • Xbox Series X – native 4K gaming and Dolby Vision support
  • PC gaming – highest performance with a capable GPU
  • Best experience on TVs with HDMI 2.1 for 4K 120Hz
Recommended Setup Tips ▸

For the best 4K performance, we recommend:

  • Use certified HDMI 2.1 cables for modern consoles/devices
  • Enable HDR in your TV settings
  • Set streaming apps to “High” or “Auto” quality
  • Use a wired connection for stable 4K playback when possible
  • Calibrate picture settings for natural colour and brightness
