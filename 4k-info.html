4K Information

4K (Ultra HD) provides four times the resolution of Full HD, giving sharper images and richer detail. Modern TVs, streaming devices, and consoles increasingly support 4K content.

Highlights:

  • Sharper image and more detail
  • HDR support for better contrast
  • Improved colour accuracy and depth
  • Upscaling improves older content

Many streaming platforms, Blu-ray players, and broadcast channels now offer 4K content. This section will be updated with full provider details soon.

More About 4K

What do I need to watch 4K?▸

You'll need a 4K-capable TV, a device or service that provides 4K content, and a fast internet connection (typically 25 Mbps or faster for streaming). Most 4K TVs released in the last few years include built-in apps that support UHD streaming.

What is HDR?▸

HDR (High Dynamic Range) enhances brightness, contrast, and colour. Common formats include HDR10, Dolby Vision, and HLG. Many new TVs support multiple HDR formats for the best possible picture.

Do all channels broadcast in 4K?▸

Not yet. Some sports channels and premium subscription services offer 4K broadcast or streaming alternatives. Freely-compatible TVs and set-top boxes are gradually expanding 4K availability in the UK market.

Is upscaling any good?▸

Modern TV processors do an impressive job upscaling HD content to 4K. While it's not true Ultra HD, good upscaling makes everyday TV look considerably sharper.
